# Original Paper Context and Critique

The 2012 paper is useful here as historical context and as a hypothesis generator, not as a template to reproduce line by line. Its strongest contribution is the intuition that evolutionary signals may differ across regions of the N-glycosylation pathway. The modern project should formalize that intuition as a robustness/evolvability architecture hypothesis and test it with multiple orthogonal evidence streams.

Key limitations to account for in any retrospective discussion include crude continental groupings, possible demographic confounding, fixed gene windows, LD-sensitive SNP p-value aggregation, ascertainment issues in array-era data, and weak assignment of candidate signals to causal genes. Selection-scan outliers should be described as candidate or hypothesis-generating unless supported by functional, fine-mapping, or replicated evidence.

The revised paper should avoid centering on a narrow reanalysis of population differentiation. Population-genetic signals can contribute evidence, but the main argument should integrate constraint, disease architecture, pathway topology, tissue specificity, trait associations, and environmental-interface biology.
