# Loci Associated With N-Glycosylation of Human Immunoglobulin G Show Pleiotropy With Autoimmune Diseases and Haematological Cancers

Citation key: `lauc_igg_nglycosylation_loci`

Authors: Lauc; Huffman; Pucic; Zgaga; et al.

Year: 2013

Journal: PLoS Genetics

Link: https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1003225

## Summary

This paper maps genetic loci associated with IgG N-glycosylation traits and reports pleiotropy with autoimmune diseases and hematological cancers. It connects antibody glycan-output variation to immune and disease biology.

## Relevance To This Project

This paper supports the prediction that downstream glycosylation variation can connect to immune and complex disease phenotypes. It is especially relevant to the interface-layer argument because IgG glycosylation modulates immune function.

## Use In Manuscript

Use as evidence that N-glycan output traits have measurable genetic architecture and immune disease relevance.

Use in the trait/interface evidence stream.

## Limitations

IgG is a specific glycoprotein and immune context. Associations may reflect immune-cell state, trans-regulatory effects, or nearby loci rather than direct adaptation of N-glycosylation enzymes.
