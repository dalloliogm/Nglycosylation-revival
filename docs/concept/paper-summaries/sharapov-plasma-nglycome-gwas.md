# Defining the Genetic Control of Human Blood Plasma N-Glycome Using Genome-Wide Association Study

Citation key: `sharapov_plasma_nglycome_gwas`

Authors: Sharapov; et al.

Year: 2019

Journal: Human Molecular Genetics

Link: https://doi.org/10.1093/hmg/ddz054

## Summary

This paper expands genome-wide association analysis of the human plasma N-glycome. It identifies replicated loci influencing plasma glycan traits and implicates both glycosylation enzymes and broader regulatory biology.

## Relevance To This Project

This strengthens the empirical case that glycan-output variation is a tractable layer of human variation. It supports the revised model in which evolvability may be found in glycan traits and regulation rather than coding-sequence acceleration.

## Use In Manuscript

Use in the glycome genetics evidence stream.

Use as support for including plasma N-glycome loci in the regulatory/trait architecture analysis.

## Limitations

Plasma N-glycome is not a direct readout of every N-glycosylation pathway gene. Interpretation requires careful mapping from glycan traits to enzymes, proteins, tissues, and loci.
