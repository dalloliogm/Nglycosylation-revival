# Glycans - The Third Revolution in Evolution

Citation key: `lauc_glycans_third_revolution`

Authors: Lauc; Kristic; Zoldos

Year: 2014

Journal: Frontiers in Genetics

Link: https://doi.org/10.3389/fgene.2014.00145

## Summary

This conceptual paper argues that glycans represent a major biological information layer in evolution. Because glycans are generated by regulated biosynthetic networks, they can produce substantial phenotypic diversity without direct one-gene-one-product templating.

The paper emphasizes glycosylation as a flexible layer between genotype, physiology, and environment.

## Relevance To This Project

This paper supports the conceptual basis for treating downstream glycan-output diversity as a plausible evolvability layer. It helps motivate why glycosylation is a strong case study rather than just another metabolic pathway.

## Use In Manuscript

Use in the introduction and discussion as conceptual support.

Use cautiously to frame, not to prove, the pathway-region hypothesis.

## Limitations

The paper is broad and conceptual. It does not directly test N-glycosylation gene classes, disease gradients, or pathway-region architecture.
