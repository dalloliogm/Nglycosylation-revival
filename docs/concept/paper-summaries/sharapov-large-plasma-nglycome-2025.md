# Genome-Wide Association Study of the Human Plasma N-Glycome Identifies Genetic Links to Liver Diseases and Anti-Inflammatory Proteins

Citation key: `sharapov_large_plasma_nglycome_2025`

Authors: Sharapov; et al.

Year: 2025

Journal: Nature Communications

Link: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-60431-y

## Summary

This recent large plasma N-glycome GWAS links glycan traits to many genetic loci and disease-relevant biology, including liver disease and anti-inflammatory proteins. It provides a high-powered modern resource for studying the genetic architecture of glycan-output variation.

## Relevance To This Project

This paper is a strong modern anchor for the regulatory/glycan-output evidence stream. It can help test whether N-glycosylation-related genes and regulatory loci connect to disease and interface phenotypes in ways consistent with the robustness/evolvability model.

## Use In Manuscript

Use as a current data-source anchor for glycome trait genetics.

Use in the evidence plan for trait/disease architecture and regulatory evolvability.

## Limitations

It is an association study, and plasma N-glycome traits are composite phenotypes. Pathway-region claims require careful locus-to-gene mapping and separation of direct glycosylation enzymes from trans-regulatory or tissue-composition effects.
