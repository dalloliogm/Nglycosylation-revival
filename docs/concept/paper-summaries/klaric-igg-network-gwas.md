# Glycosylation of Immunoglobulin G Is Regulated by a Large Network of Genes Pleiotropic With Inflammatory Diseases

Citation key: `klarić_igg_network_gwas`

Authors: Klaric; Tsepilov; Stanton; Mangino; Sikka; et al.

Year: 2020

Journal: Science Advances

Link: https://doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.aax0301

## Summary

This large IgG glycosylation GWAS shows that antibody glycan traits are regulated by a distributed genetic network. It reports overlap between IgG glycosylation loci and inflammatory disease biology, connecting glycan-output variation to immune and inflammatory phenotypes.

## Relevance To This Project

This is a high-priority evidence source for the downstream/interface and complex-disease parts of the thesis. It supports the idea that glycosylation variation is linked to immune regulation and inflammatory disease through a broad genetic architecture.

## Use In Manuscript

Use in the trait-association and disease-architecture evidence streams.

Use as modern support for regulatory evolvability and immune-interface relevance.

## Limitations

IgG glycosylation is a specific immune glycoprotein context. The loci may involve direct glycosylation enzymes, trans regulators, immune-cell biology, or correlated disease processes. Do not treat these loci as direct proof of adaptive evolution.
