# Multivariate Genome-Wide Association Study Identifies New Loci Associated With IgG N-Glycosylation

Citation key: `shadrina_multivariate_igg_nglycosylation`

Authors: Shadrina; et al.

Year: 2021

Journal: Frontiers in Immunology

Link: https://doi.org/10.3389/fimmu.2021.707547

## Summary

This multivariate GWAS identifies additional loci associated with IgG N-glycosylation. It reinforces that antibody glycan-output traits are influenced by distributed genetic architecture.

## Relevance To This Project

This paper supports the regulatory and immune-interface layer of the thesis. It suggests that downstream glycan phenotypes can be genetically complex and connected to immune function.

## Use In Manuscript

Use in the IgG glycosylation and immune trait evidence stream.

Use to support the idea that glycan-output variation is distributed across direct and indirect genetic regulators.

## Limitations

IgG glycosylation is not the whole N-glycosylation pathway. GWAS associations require careful locus-to-gene interpretation and cannot be treated as proof of adaptation.
