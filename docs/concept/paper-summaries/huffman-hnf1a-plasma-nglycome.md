# Genomics Meets Glycomics - The First GWAS Study of Human N-Glycome Identifies HNF1A as a Master Regulator of Plasma Protein Fucosylation

Citation key: `huffman_hnf1a_plasma_nglycome`

Authors: Huffman; Knezevic; Vitart; Kattla; Adamczyk; et al.

Year: 2011

Journal: PLoS Genetics

Link: https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1001256

## Summary

This was an early genome-wide association study of the human plasma N-glycome. It identified genetic loci associated with variation in plasma protein glycosylation, including `HNF1A`, a regulatory locus linked to fucosylation patterns.

The study demonstrated that glycan-output traits can be mapped genetically and that important loci may be regulatory rather than only direct glycosylation enzymes.

## Relevance To This Project

This paper is important because it supports glycan-output variation as a modern evidence stream for the architecture model. It also shows that regulatory genes can shape downstream glycan phenotypes, which fits the revised thesis.

## Use In Manuscript

Use in the regulatory/glycome genetics evidence section.

Use as an example that glycan trait variation is measurable and genetically tractable.

## Limitations

Plasma N-glycome is a composite phenotype from many proteins and tissues. Locus-to-gene assignment and pathway-region interpretation need care.
