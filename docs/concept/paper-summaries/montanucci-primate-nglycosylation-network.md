# Molecular Evolution and Network-Level Analysis of the N-Glycosylation Metabolic Pathway Across Primates

Citation key: `montanucci_primate_nglycosylation_network`

Authors: Montanucci; Laayouni; Dall'Olio; Bertranpetit

Year: 2011

Journal: Molecular Biology and Evolution

Link: https://doi.org/10.1093/molbev/msq259

## Summary

This paper analyzes molecular evolution of N-glycosylation genes across primates in relation to pathway/network structure. It tests whether genes in a pathway that produces cell-surface glycan diversity show protein-coding evidence of adaptation and whether evolutionary rates relate to pathway position or connectivity.

The main result is conservative: N-glycosylation genes are strongly conserved, with no clear evidence for protein-coding positive selection across primate divergence. The paper finds that network features, especially connectivity and pathway position, are associated with purifying constraint.

## Relevance To This Project

This is a direct predecessor. It shows that N-glycosylation is already an established target for pathway-level evolutionary analysis, but it also warns against expecting downstream evolvability to appear as faster coding-sequence evolution.

For the new paper, this supports a refined model: core and downstream enzymes may both be coding-constrained, while evolvability may appear in regulatory control, glycan-output diversity, tissue deployment, disease architecture, and trait associations.

## Use In Manuscript

Use in introduction and prior-work framing.

Use as historical context for why the new paper should not center on dN/dS or protein-coding positive selection.

## Limitations

The analysis is comparative coding-sequence evolution across primates, not modern human constraint, disease, regulatory, or glycome trait architecture. Network conclusions depend on pathway encoding and available annotations at the time.
