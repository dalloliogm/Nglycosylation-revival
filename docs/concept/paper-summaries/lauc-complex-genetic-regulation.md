# Complex Genetic Regulation of Protein Glycosylation

Citation key: `lauc_complex_genetic_regulation`

Authors: Lauc; et al.

Year: 2010

Journal: Molecular BioSystems

Link: https://doi.org/10.1039/B910377E

## Summary

This paper presents early evidence that human glycosylation traits are under complex genetic regulation. It treats glycan-output profiles as measurable phenotypes influenced by multiple genes and regulatory processes.

The work helps establish glycomics as an empirical layer for studying how genetic variation translates into glycan diversity.

## Relevance To This Project

This paper supports using glycan-output phenotypes as evidence for downstream or regulatory evolvability. It helps bridge from a conceptual gene-environment glycosylation model to measurable human variation.

## Use In Manuscript

Use as background for the emergence of glycome genetics as a data stream.

Use in methods planning to justify glycome GWAS as relevant to pathway architecture.

## Limitations

The study predates larger glycome GWAS and current fine-mapping resources. Use it as historical support, with later GWAS papers carrying more empirical weight.
