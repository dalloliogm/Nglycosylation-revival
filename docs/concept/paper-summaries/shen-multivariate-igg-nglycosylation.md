# Multivariate Discovery and Replication of Five Novel Loci Associated With Immunoglobulin G N-Glycosylation

Citation key: `shen_multivariate_igg_nglycosylation`

Authors: Shen; et al.

Year: 2017

Journal: Nature Communications

Link: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-017-00453-3

## Summary

This study uses multivariate GWAS methods to identify and replicate loci associated with IgG N-glycosylation traits. It expands the set of genetic loci linked to antibody glycan variation and shows that multivariate modeling can improve discovery for correlated glycan traits.

## Relevance To This Project

This paper supports the idea that glycan-output variation is genetically tractable and relevant to immune biology. It belongs in the regulatory/trait layer of the revised thesis, where evolvability is expected to appear in glycan traits and immune-interface phenotypes rather than necessarily in coding-sequence acceleration.

## Use In Manuscript

Use in the glycome genetics and immune-interface evidence stream.

Use as methodological support for treating correlated glycan traits as a structured phenotype set.

## Limitations

The phenotype is IgG-specific. Association loci require careful gene assignment, and the results do not by themselves prove adaptation or pathway-region architecture.
